Modern power grid operators must schedule maintenance under increasingly complex constraints, balancing security of supply, financial costs, and socio-environmental externalities. While combinatorial optimization solves for feasibility, standard objective functions often collapse these heterogeneous criteria into a single scalar, obscuring critical trade-offs. This paper proposes a robust Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) framework to select maintenance schedules from a pool of solutions. The Weighted Ordered Weighted Averaging (WOWA) operator is employed to model operational priorities where grid safety strictly precedes economic or strategic goals. A key theoretical contribution is a transformation that maps WOWA into an equivalent high-dimensional OWA on a unified simplex, enabling the use of Linear Programming to compute a stability radius for each candidate, identifying solutions that remain dominant under the widest range of preference uncertainty. Applied to the ROADEF/EURO 2020 Challenge dataset, the framework reveals that mathematically optimal solutions often lack structural robustness, and identifies distinct schedules best suited to conservative, neutral, and sustainability-oriented operational profiles.
